  • Training

    • Led by an industry-expert instructor.

    The development and implementation of our training program curriculum is being led by Christine Tsui, a veteran in the industry with over 25 years of experience. She is building on top of the foundation laid by Pennie Sangerman, our pilot trianing instructor. Learn more about Christine on our team page.

    Keeping our designer clients in mind.

    One of our priorities is ensuring that the curriculum is geared towards meeting the essential apparel manufacturing needs of local designers. We will continue involving designers in the further development of our curriculum to ensure we are trianing our students in relevant, needed skills.

    Tailored towards our students.

    The class is being designed specifically for low-income immigrants and refugees and addresses some of their unique challenges around limited English proficiency, lack of transportation, and need for childcare.

    Muses works with students lacking strong English fluency through covering industry- relevant vocabulary and focusing on visual demonstrations over verbal communication. We are actively looking for a solution to help our students with transportation costs and childcare.

    Our goal is to create a space that makes students feel safe and supported. We strive for a classroom culture with strong interpersonal relationships and filled with laughter!

    Investing in future careers.

    While we cannot guarantee immediate employment upon graduation, we will play an active role in connecting our students with employment opportunities. Their career paths may include working for designers or manufacturing houses, starting their own business, or joining our own Muses production studio. Our priority will be on ensuring fair living wages and good working conditions.

    Our curriculum

    Our pilot program will ran for 5 months total, ending in November 2013. Currently, we are focusing on building our full training program launching in early 2015, which will cover the following topics:

    • Apparel Production Basics: Students master the basics of fitting, patternmaking, cutting, measuring, production lifecycle, machinery, and construction.

    • Advanced Sewing & Construction: Students create ready-to-wear and tailored garment by executing on advanced pattern designs.
